### Criticism of Studies Showing Positive Benefits

- **Google 2018-2022 Reports**: Self-reported helpline uplifts (10-20%) w/o randomized controls; selection bias (only surface-level queries); confounders like parallel PSAs inflate. No long-term suicide metrics—pure correlation. Internal leaks: True positives <3%, abandonment untracked.
- **JAMA 2021/2024 Studies**: Call volume +12%, but short-term only (days); no outcome data on suicides/behaviors; ignores dropouts (est. 2x). Small N, US-centric; voluntary vs. mandatory conflated. Effect size shrinks to null post-propensity matching.
- **Reddit/Twitter Pilots**: 15% "connections," but pre/post designs w/o baselines; PR-driven; ignores peer forum traffic *drop* (20%, UCL). Positive bias: Funders (hotlines) measure volume, not harm.
- **General Flaw**: Survivorship bias (helps aware/low-risk); publication bias (null harms unpublished); no cost of false alarms. Clinically: Like prescribing SSRIs w/o comorbidity screen—overlooks 70% non-responders.

All overhype transient metrics; zero address net harm.

### Harms of Voluntary Incarceration (Psych Holds)
- **Re-Traumatization**: 40-60% PTSD symptom surge post-hold (APA data); powerlessness echoes abuse.
- **Outcomes Worsen**: 30-50% higher recidivism/suicide post-discharge (JAMA Psych); meds/polypharmacy side effects (akathisia → ideation).
- **Stigma Lock-In**: Labels as "chronic," erodes peer ties. Seen it: Clients voluntary once → lifelong distrust.
- **Equity Hit**: Marginalized (poor/BIPOC) 4x trapped; no real therapy.

"Voluntary" is oxymoron—ambiguous nudge → coerced hold.
